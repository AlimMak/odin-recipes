Crispy Friend Chicken

Savory and Tastful chicken

Fried chicken is a beloved dish that has captured the hearts and taste buds of people all over the world. With its crispy, golden-brown exterior and tender, juicy meat, it offers a delightful sensory experience. The process of frying chicken involves coating the pieces in a seasoned batter or flour mixture before immersing them in hot oil, creating a deliciously crunchy texture that contrasts with the succulent meat inside. Whether enjoyed as a comforting home-cooked meal, a picnic favorite, or a staple at fast-food chains, fried chicken continues to be a timeless classic that brings people together through its irresistible flavor.

The art of making fried chicken spans cultures and culinary traditions, resulting in a rich diversity of recipes and variations. From the iconic Southern-style fried chicken in the United States, with its signature blend of herbs and spices, to Korean fried chicken known for its crispy exterior and sticky-sweet glaze, each rendition offers a unique twist on this popular dish. Accompanied by a range of side dishes, such as fluffy biscuits, coleslaw, or hot sauce, fried chicken satisfies cravings and creates a memorable dining experience. Its universal appeal and ability to adapt to different flavors and cooking techniques have solidified fried chicken's place as a beloved global culinary delight.

Ingredients:

  • 4lbs chicken cut into pieces
  • 1 cup buttermilk
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour for coating
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 quarts vegetable oil for frying

Steps:

  1. Take your cut up chicken pieces and skin them if you prefer

  2. Put the flour in a large plastic bag (let the amount of chicken you are cooking dictate the amount of flour you use). Season the flour with paprika, salt and pepper to taste (paprika helps to brown the chicken).

  3. Dip chicken pieces in buttermilk then, a few at a time, put them in the bag with the flour, seal the bag and shake to coat well.

  4. Place the coated chicken on a cookie sheet or tray, and cover with a clean dish towel or waxed paper. LET SIT UNTIL THE FLOUR IS OF A PASTE-LIKE CONSISTENCY. THIS IS CRUCIAL!

  5. Fill a large skillet (cast iron is best) about 1/3 to 1/2 full with vegetable oil. Heat until VERY hot.

  6. Put in as many chicken pieces as the skillet can hold. Brown the chicken in HOT oil on both sides.

  7. When browned, reduce heat and cover skillet; let cook for 30 minutes (the chicken will be cooked through but not crispy). Remove cover, raise heat again, and continue to fry until crispy.

  8. Drain the fried chicken on paper towels. Depending on how much chicken you have, you may have to fry in a few shifts. Keep the finished chicken in a slightly warm oven while preparing the rest.
